Work

One of the interesting facts I have realized through time is that most of my friends or family don't exactly know about my job, or at least the job title. It's quite difficult most of the time to tell other people exactly what I do as an Embedded Design Engineer (to be more specific besides electrical engineer) or what is the job about. Sometimes I just tell people I work as an Electrical Engineer because it might sound easier to be visualized. Around 80% of the times when I say that, people assume that I work as an electrician, the other 20% of the time they will think of an IT job if I mention that I could also program somethings with the computer. So, in this page, I will give out a funny brief description of my job below for those who is not in the field.

WHAT IS MY JOB ABOUT? :D

An Embedded Design Engineer is like a superhero who makes electronic devices do amazing things! They design circuit boards, or "superhero hideouts," called PCBs, where all the electronic parts live. Then, they use their special powers to analyze these electronic devices and make sure they are working properly. Finally, they use their super computer skills to program the devices, or give them "superpowers" using embedded software. It's like giving a robot the ability to fly or a phone the power to talk to you! All in all, being an Embedded Design Engineer is a lot of fun and a great way to make a real impact on the world around you. First heart-shape LED matrix I made

A circuit board, or PCB, is like the brain of an electronic device. It's a thin piece of material, usually made of fiberglass or plastic, that has lots of tiny pathways, or circuits, etched onto it. Think of it like a city map where each pathway is a street and each electronic component is a building. A lot of "cookies" PCB The Embedded Design Engineer's job is to design and lay out this city, making sure that all the buildings are in the right place, and that the streets connect them all together. It's like playing with a really cool and advanced version of legos, but instead of building houses, you're building electronic devices! My thesis project PCB :D My proud thesis project :D

As related to hardware tasks. Imagine I use special computer programs to make a blueprint for the device, just like how an architect makes a blueprint for a building. Then I use this blueprint to create the circuit board or called as PCB. Once the PCB is ready, I will assemble the electronic components on it, similar to how you would assemble a puzzle. I will test the circuit board to ensure it functions properly. Programming on actual hardware

Think of the previous part as I was creating an electronic toy. After assembling it, I give it special powers using magic spells, called embedded software. I write these spells using a special language, like how wizards use Latin to cast spells. Once the spells are written, I use a magic wand, called a programmer, to put the spells into the toy's memory, like how Hermione puts books into her beaded bag. Then, I test the spells to make sure they work correctly and don't have any bugs, just like how Harry Potter tests the spells he learned in class. If everything works well, the toy is ready to be played with, just like how a toy comes out of a box. The toy can now do all kinds of cool tricks, like talk to you, take pictures, or drive around! All of this is possible because of the embedded software, the magic spells that the Embedded Design Engineer has cast into it.
